Baked Cajun chicken is a straightforward oven-roasted dish built around bold seasoning and minimal prep. Chicken breasts or thighs are coated in a blend of Cajun spices, olive oil, and garlic, then baked until juicy inside with a lightly crisp exterior.

The Cajun seasoning brings layered heat and smokiness, typically combining paprika, cayenne, garlic powder, onion powder, oregano, thyme, salt, and black pepper. As the chicken bakes, the spices bloom in the oil, forming a flavorful crust while the meat stays tender.

This recipe is designed for simplicity and consistency. Everything cooks in one pan, making it easy to prepare for weeknight dinners or meal prep. The finished chicken pairs well with rice, roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or a simple starch that balances the spice.

The result is a high-protein, oven-friendly dish with strong flavor, low effort, and flexible sides.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breasts or chicken thighs
  • Cajun seasoning
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic powder
  • Paprika
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400F (205C).
  2. Pat the chicken dry with paper towels and place it in a baking dish or on a rimmed sheet pan.
  3. In a small bowl, mix Cajun seasoning, paprika, salt, black pepper, and garlic with olive oil to form a paste.
  4. Rub the seasoning mixture evenly over both sides of the chicken.
  5. Arrange the chicken in a single layer, leaving space between pieces.
  6. Bake for 20–25 minutes for breasts or 30–35 minutes for thighs, until the internal temperature reaches 165F (74C).
  7. Optional: switch the oven to broil for 2–3 minutes at the end to lightly crisp the surface.
  8. Remove from the oven and let rest for 5 minutes before serving.
