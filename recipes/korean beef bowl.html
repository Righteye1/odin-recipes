Korean Beef Bowl

Homepage

A Korean beef bowl is a simple, fast dish built around savory-sweet beef served over warm rice. The beef is typically ground or thinly sliced and cooked quickly in a skillet, making it well suited for weeknight meals while still delivering bold flavor.

The sauce combines soy sauce, brown sugar, garlic, and sesame oil, creating a balance of saltiness, sweetness, and richness. Fresh ginger is often added for mild heat and depth. As the beef cooks, the sauce reduces slightly and coats the meat, producing a glossy, aromatic finish.

The bowl is assembled over steamed white rice or jasmine rice, which absorbs the sauce and anchors the dish. Common toppings include sliced green onions, sesame seeds, and sometimes sauteed vegetables such as bell peppers, carrots, or spinach. For added heat, gochujang or red pepper flakes can be mixed into the sauce or served on the side.

The result is a compact, protein-rich meal with strong umami flavor, minimal prep time, and flexible ingredients, making it easy to adapt for both casual home cooking and meal prep.

Ingredients

  • Ground beef
  • Soy sauce
  • Brown sugar
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Sesame oil
  • White rice
  • Green onions
  • Sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Cook the rice according to package instructions and keep warm.
  2. In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, brown sugar, minced garlic, grated ginger, and sesame oil until the sugar is dissolved.
  3. Heat a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook, breaking it up as it browns. Continue cooking until no pink remains.
  4. Drain excess fat if needed, then reduce heat to medium. Pour the sauce over the beef and stir to coat evenly.
  5. Simmer for 2–4 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly and fully coat the meat.
  6. If using vegetables, add them during the simmer and cook just until tender.
  7. Spoon the beef over warm rice. Finish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds.
